Week 13¶

Alex Wagner, 11/30/2016

The season is wrapping up, and with this week's results a majority of the teams are still in contest for the playoffs, but few have good odds. This report uses our previous performance and current standings to predict each team's chances of making the championship, winning the playoffs, or getting last place. This is accomplished through simulating millions of matchups based on the remaining season schedule.

def simulate_matchup(week, records, team1, team2):
    r1 = records[team1]
    r2 = records[team2]
    team1_mean = r1[2]/(week - 1)
    team2_mean = r2[2]/(week - 1)
    team1_score = round(np.random.normal(team1_mean, team1_mean / 10),2)
    team2_score = round(np.random.normal(team2_mean, team2_mean / 10),2)
    if team1_score > team2_score:
        a = 1
        b = 0
        winner = team1
        loser = team2
    elif team2_score > team1_score:
        a = 0
        b = 1
        winner = team2
        loser = team1
    else:
        return simulate_matchup(week, records, team1, team2)
    team1_result = (r1[0] + a, r1[1] + b, r1[2] + team1_score)
    team2_result = (r2[0] + b, r2[1] + a, r2[2] + team2_score)
    return { 'winner': winner,
             'loser': loser,
             'values': (team1_result, team2_result) }

def simulate_season_rank():
    new_records = records.copy()
    for week in range(COMPLETED_WEEKS + 1,15):
        for matchup in schedule[week]:
            r = simulate_matchup(week, new_records, *matchup)
            new_records[matchup[0]], new_records[matchup[1]] = r['values']
    r = new_records
    return sorted(r, key=lambda x: (r[x][0], r[x][2]), reverse=True)

def simulate_n(chunk_size):
    outcomes = {team: [] for team in records.keys()}
    playoff_wins = Counter()
    last_place_finish = Counter()
    
    for _ in range(chunk_size):
        r = simulate_ROS()
        playoff_wins[r[0]] += 1
        last_place_finish[r[1]] += 1
        for i, team in enumerate(r[2], start=1):
            outcomes[team].append(i)
    return playoff_wins, last_place_finish, outcomes

    results = p.map(simulate_n, [chunk_size] * (trials // chunk_size))

for r in results:
    playoff_wins += r[0]
    last_place_finish += r[1]
    for team, o in r[2].items():
        outcomes[team] += o
        
for team in outcomes:
    outcomes[team] = np.array(outcomes[team])

Discussion¶

The Final Countdown¶

There are two weeks left in the regular season, and the majority of teams now have a very good sense of their post-season fate.

A Sure Bet¶

Congratulations to Black Eagle, Thundering Typhoons, and the Deep Space Niners. These teams have very good odds to make the playoffs, and don't need to worry much over the home stretch. At this point, Susanna's Black Eagle is the team to watch, currently projected as most likely to win the championship on the backs of Adam Thielen and Mark Ingram (38.82% of outcomes). Due to the emergence of Alvin Kamara and Robby Anderson among the fantasy elite, however, Malachi's Thundering Typhoons has scored no less than 110 points since week 8 and is my personal favorite to win the championship this year.

Strong Contenders¶

Remarkably, Lee's Assgard Mjlnirlicious is the strongest contender for the #4 seed in the playoffs. Despite a weaker win-loss record compared to No Tengo Idea, an easier week 14 matchup and a league-best point total makes them the current favorite to make the playoffs.

So You're Telling Me There's a Chance¶

Despite the goods odds for the aforementioned teams to make the playoffs, there is still some room for the runners-up to grab a Griffith Bowl slot for themselves. Good luck to Always Blue! a real playoff threat, only one win and 5 points behind Assgard Mjlnirlicious with two weeks to go in the season. Zach's House Lannister technically deserves a mention here as well, as they have a (very slim) path to the playoffs at well (0.80% of outcomes).

Race to the Bottom¶

On the other end of our league, the contest to not finish last is also largely decided. Things are looking particularly grim for A Team Has No Name, Lost and Confused, and Hamm's Hitters, each of whom are locked into the bottom 4, save for the 0.09% margin for Hamm's Hitters. Password is Taco is in very real danger here as well, ending in the bottom 4 in 81% of outcomes.

Stay Vigilant¶

This doesn't mean that our inaugural Toilet Bowl champion Blistering Barnacles should be unconcerned. If the rest of the season goes crappy, Obi's team has a very real chance of restoring the Toilet Bowl trophy to its original resting place on his office wall. As week 14 is a battle of the Captian Haddock Curses (and the outcome is nearly a foregone conclusion), this week's matchup against Katie's Password is Taco may be the most important remaining game of the regular season.

Time for a Change¶

Cody's Lost and Confused is in dismal disrepair after avoiding the waiver wire almost every week (9 total acquisitions) and holding the record for the most inactive players started in league history (12 inactive players over 7 weeks, 14 total roster moves). If there's ever been a clear-cut favorite for the golden toilet seat, it's this team. The seat may yet be avoidable with some savvy picks and an eye on winning in weeks 15 and 16--pay close attention to the waivers in the coming weeks!
