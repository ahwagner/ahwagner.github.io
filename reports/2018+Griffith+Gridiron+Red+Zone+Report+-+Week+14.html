2018 Griffith Gridiron Red Zone Report¶

Week 14¶

Alex Wagner, 12/04/2018

Hello all and welcome to our traditional Griffith Gridiron fantasy football seasonal reports. The first report this year comes with only a week left in the regular season, but there are still hopefuls to root for (or against) and close matchups that will affect their postseason aspirations! The final week of the regular season is upon us, and through week 13 there remain a few postseason slots in contention. This report uses our previous performance and current standings to predict each team's chances of making the championship, winning the playoffs, or getting last place. This is accomplished through simulating millions of matchups based on the remaining season schedule.

def simulate_matchup(week, records, team1, team2):
    r1 = records[team1]
    r2 = records[team2]
    team1_mean = r1[2]/(week - 1)
    team2_mean = r2[2]/(week - 1)
    team1_score = round(np.random.normal(team1_mean, team1_mean / 10),2)
    team2_score = round(np.random.normal(team2_mean, team2_mean / 10),2)
    if team1_score > team2_score:
        a = 1
        b = 0
        winner = team1
        loser = team2
    elif team2_score > team1_score:
        a = 0
        b = 1
        winner = team2
        loser = team1
    else:
        return simulate_matchup(week, records, team1, team2)
    team1_result = (r1[0] + a, r1[1] + b, r1[2] + team1_score)
    team2_result = (r2[0] + b, r2[1] + a, r2[2] + team2_score)
    return { 'winner': winner,
             'loser': loser,
             'values': (team1_result, team2_result) }

def simulate_season_rank():
    new_records = records.copy()
    for week in range(COMPLETED_WEEKS + 1,15):
        for matchup in schedule[week]:
            r = simulate_matchup(week, new_records, *matchup)
            new_records[matchup[0]], new_records[matchup[1]] = r['values']
    r = new_records
    return sorted(r, key=lambda x: (r[x][0], r[x][2]), reverse=True)

def simulate_n(chunk_size):
    outcomes = {team: [] for team in records.keys()}
    playoff_wins = Counter()
    last_place_finish = Counter()
    
    for _ in range(chunk_size):
        r = simulate_ROS()
        playoff_wins[r[0]] += 1
        last_place_finish[r[1]] += 1
        for i, team in enumerate(r[2], start=1):
            outcomes[team].append(i)
    return playoff_wins, last_place_finish, outcomes

    playoff_wins += r[0]
    last_place_finish += r[1]

        outcomes[team] += o

Discussion¶

The Final Countdown¶

With only one week left in the regular season, the majority of teams now have a very good sense of their post-season fate. However, every team still maintains a shot at the postseason, for good or ill.

A Sure Bet¶

Congratulations to the Blistering Barnacles, Deep Space Niners, and Evansville Crimson Giants. These teams are a solid lock for the playoffs, and can rest easy in week 14. The 2018 Barnacles are especially inspiring, as Obi has turned around a 5-9 2015 season and postseason Toilet Bowl trophy into a true Cinderella story, currently projected as most likely to win the championship (51.17% of outcomes) on breakout seasons for both Aaron Jones and The Chicago Bears D/ST. Conversely, despite a strong early- and mid-season, Malachi and the Thundering Typhoons have lost their last 2 games, and now face likely back-to-back losses from their Captain-Haddock-themed counterpart.

So You're Telling Me There's a Chance¶

The Hitters technically have a chance to leverage the Typhoons plight into a playoff slot, but after losing to the lackluster Lannisters in week 13 the odds are long (< 0.01% of outcomes). Accomplishing this would require an extremely unlikely point differential (+148.7 points) against the Typhoons to eclipse their total points-for in the final game of the regular season. Of course, this did happen at least once this season already (Barnacles outscored Taco by 167.4 points in Week 10), but we're largely talking about miracles at this scale.

Race to the Bottom¶

On the other end of our league, the contest to not finish last is also largely decided. Things are looking particularly grim for Password is Taco, No Tengo Idea, and Assgard Mjölnirlicious, each of whom are essentially locked into the bottom 4, with less than a 3% margin of escape.

I'm not Nervous. You're Nervous.¶

Somehow, all of our teams that are not in contention for the championship can still find themselves heading to the Toilet Bowl after week 14! Of these, House Lannister has the slimmest chance of ending up in the bottom 4, but the perfect storm could drop Zach into the loathsome position of defending his team from acquiring the golden toilet seat (3.21% of outcomes).

Stay Vigilant¶

This means that last year's Toilet Bowl champion A Team Has No Name should be concerned. Erica's team has a very real chance of retaining the Toilet Bowl trophy if week 14 goes crappy (51.32% of outcomes with a bottom-4 finish). Maintaining only a single-game lead over Black Eagle and House Lannister and >100 points behind them, the No Names are depending on at least one of those teams to lose (or their own win) in week 14. Otherwise there is a very real possibility that the Toilet Bowl trophy seat sits at its current resting place for another year. Amazingly, Always Blue! also retains a substantial chance of postseason woes, despite their current standing at #6 overall. Going up against the stronger Eagles, Blue! shares many of the same concerns as the No Names in week 14. These four teams are in a neck-and-neck contest to see who will have to take the plunge in the postseason, but the No Names are the foremost candidate in the set.

Notably, last year's champion Black Eagle has fallen far from their rookie performance, ending in the bottom 4 in 31.79% of outcomes after going 1-5 in the second half of the season. Susanna is in a must-win situation and needs to right the ship quickly if she hopes to avoid the depths, as her primary competition House Lannister has gone .500 over that same stretch and is strongly favored to win against Password is Taco in week 14.

Time for a Change¶

Speaking of, the terrible Tacos are in dismal disrepair after Peter avoided the waiver wire almost every week (7 total acquisitions) and set a new record for the most inactive players started in league history (13 inactive players over 9 weeks, 13 total roster moves). If there's ever been a clear-cut favorite for the golden toilet seat, it's this team. The dubious distinction may yet be avoidable with some savvy picks and an eye on winning in weeks 15 and 16--pay close attention to the waivers in the coming weeks!
